We overestimate the impact of big decisions and underestimate the impact of small ones.

The decision to write every day seems small. The decision to read for 20 minutes before bed seems small. The decision to take a walk at lunch seems small.

But compound them over a year, five years, a decade — and they define who you become.

The trick isn't to make better big decisions. It's to make better small ones, more consistently.

What small decision could you make today that your future self would thank you for?
